# YouTube Titles and Descriptions Framework

## Titles

The title does one job: get the click. It works alongside the thumbnail — the thumbnail stops the scroll; the title converts the curious viewer into a click. Both must work together.

### Title principles

**Be specific, not clever.** Specific titles outperform clever ones. "How I Lost 20 Pounds in 12 Weeks Without Tracking Calories" outperforms "My Weight Loss Journey" on every metric.

**Lead with the most important word.** YouTube truncates titles in the feed — especially on mobile. Put the keyword or the highest-value word at the start.

**The title is a promise.** It must describe what the video actually delivers. Clickbait that doesn't deliver trains viewers to not trust you. One bad misleading title can cost more than the initial views gained.

### Title formats that work

**The How-To**
*"How to [achieve specific result] [optional: without/even if/in X time]"*
- "How to Write a Cover Letter That Gets You Interviews"
- "How to Sleep Better Without Medication"

**The Number List**
*"[Number] [Things/Ways/Reasons] [specific topic]"*
- "7 Mistakes New Investors Make (And How to Avoid Them)"
- "5 Things I Wish I Knew Before Starting a Business"

**The Result**
*"I [did specific thing] for [time period]. Here's What Happened."*
- "I Cold-Called 100 People in 30 Days. Here's What Happened."
- "I Ate One Meal a Day for 6 Months. Here's What I Learned."

**The Challenge / Counterintuitive**
*"[Common belief] Is Wrong. Here's Why."*
- "You're Doing Productivity Wrong. Here's the Fix."
- "Everything You Know About Email Marketing Is Outdated."

**The Why**
*"Why [thing] [consequence]"*
- "Why Most YouTube Channels Fail in Year One"
- "Why Nobody Is Watching Your Videos (And How to Fix It)"

**The Secret / What Nobody Tells You**
*"The [thing] Nobody Talks About in [topic]"*
- "The Skill Nobody Talks About in Sales"
- "What Nobody Tells You About Freelancing"

### Title length

**50–70 characters** is the target. This is what displays fully on most devices. Under 50 and you may be leaving valuable information out. Over 70 and it will be truncated in the feed — put the key information first.

### What to avoid in titles

- ALL CAPS (looks like spam)
- Excessive punctuation (?!?!)
- Vague promises ("You Need to See This")
- The bait-and-switch (title that doesn't match the video)
- Starting with a low-value word ("So I Decided to..." / "A Conversation With...")

## Descriptions

YouTube descriptions are underused. Most creators write two sentences and call it done. A well-written description improves search ranking, gives viewers context, and converts viewers into subscribers and customers.

### Description structure

**First 150 characters (above the fold)**
This is what appears in search results and below the video before the viewer clicks "Show more." It must stand alone as a compelling one or two sentences that describe what the video offers.

*"I tested 10 productivity methods over 6 months. Here are the 3 that actually stuck — and why the rest failed."*

**Full description body (300–500 words)**

Section 1 — Expand on the video (2–3 sentences): What the video covers, who it's for, and why it matters. Write this as if the viewer hasn't watched the video yet — it should sell the watch.

Section 2 — Timestamps: If the video is over 8 minutes, include timestamps. They improve watch time (viewers can navigate to what they want) and YouTube surfaces them in search results.

```
00:00 Intro
01:23 The biggest mistake
04:45 What actually works
08:30 Step-by-step breakdown
12:00 Results and takeaways
```

Section 3 — Resources mentioned: Any tools, books, links, or resources mentioned in the video. This is where affiliate links go (clearly labeled).

Section 4 — About this channel: 2–3 sentences on what the channel covers and what a subscriber can expect. Include subscribe CTA here.

Section 5 — Keywords / hashtags: 3–5 hashtags at the end. YouTube uses these for categorization. Use them naturally — the description text itself is more important for search than the hashtags.

### Keywords in descriptions

YouTube is a search engine. People find videos by searching. Include the 2–3 main keywords the video is about naturally in the first 100 words of the description. Don't stuff — write naturally, but make sure the core topic appears early.

### Description template

```
[First 150 characters — standalone summary that sells the video]

[2–3 sentences expanding on what the video covers and who it's for]

TIMESTAMPS:
00:00 [Section name]
[XX:XX] [Section name]
...

RESOURCES MENTIONED:
- [Resource name]: [link]
- [Resource name]: [link]

ABOUT THIS CHANNEL:
[2–3 sentences on what the channel covers and what to expect]
Subscribe for [specific outcome] — I post every [cadence].

[2–5 hashtags]
```
